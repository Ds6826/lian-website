Heartwood proves who could see it. Lians proves when you knew it.

Both products take agent-memory governance seriously. Heartwood centers policy-gated recall, signed provenance, and key-destruction proof. Lians centers bitemporal facts, deterministic supersession, point-in-time reconstruction, and audit state at a requested instant.

Heartwood has a real governance thesis.

Heartwood documents policy enforcement before ranking, tenant isolation, signed provenance, a hash-chained audit log, and hard erasure that destroys a per-subject key while following deletion lineage into derived embeddings. Its MCP server is fail-closed by default and exposes a read-only subset unless write and erasure tools are explicitly allowed.

Those are meaningful controls. This comparison does not treat governance as a checkbox or claim that one product replaces the other.

Decision guide

Choose the proof your system must produce.

Choose Heartwood when the primary question is whether a principal was permitted to see a memory, whether the result carries valid provenance, and whether erasure produced a subject-level key-destruction proof.

Choose Lians when the primary question is what the agent knew at a historical instant, which revision was valid then, whether later information contaminated retrieval, and whether the complete knowledge state can be reconstructed.

For systems with both requirements, evaluate the products as complementary control planes: governance at recall and temporal truth in the memory record.
