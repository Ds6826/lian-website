Tamper-evident audit chain

Verify the record, don't just trust it.

Every write, recall, supersession, and erasure appends a row to a SHA-256 hash chain. Each row commits to the prior hash, so altering any record breaks verification.

GET /v1/admin/audit/verify returns {"chain_valid": …}. /v1/admin/audit/export produces a complete examinable log (NDJSON or CSV). The algorithm is open source.

Crypto-shred erasure

Erase data without erasing evidence.

Content is encrypted under a per-subject key. POST /v1/erase destroys that key — all content under it becomes permanently unrecoverable, including in backups, with no re-index.

The audit chain stores only hashes, so it survives intact. /v1/erase/status confirms the destruction timestamp; a signed erasure certificate is available.

Data residency

The data never leaves your perimeter.

Self-hosted, Lians runs on your Postgres and Redis on your infrastructure. The master encryption key resolves from your KMS (AWS, Azure, or Vault) and never touches vendor infrastructure. Air-gap mode hard-fails any configuration that would send data externally.
