Tamper-evident audit chain

Verify the record,
don't just trust it.

Every memory write, recall, supersession, and erasure appends a row to a SHA-256 hash chain. Each row commits to the prior hash, so altering any record breaks verification.

GET /v1/admin/audit/verify returns {"chain_valid": true/false}. GET /v1/admin/audit/export produces a complete examinable log (NDJSON or CSV). The verification algorithm is open source — your engineers can audit it.

Crypto-shred erasure

Erase data without
erasing evidence.

Content is encrypted under a per-subject data-encryption key. POST /v1/erase destroys that key — all content under it becomes permanently unrecoverable, including in backups, with no re-index required.

The audit chain stores only hashes, never personal data, so it survives the erasure intact. GET /v1/erase/status?subject_id=… confirms the destruction timestamp; a signed erasure certificate is available for GDPR / HIPAA workflows.

Data residency

The data never leaves
your perimeter.

Self-hosted, Lians runs on your Postgres and Redis on your infrastructure. The master encryption key resolves from your KMS (AWS, Azure, or Vault) and never touches vendor infrastructure. Air-gap mode hard-fails any configuration that would send data externally.
