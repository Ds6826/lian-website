Why audit matters

Storage is not the moat.

Recall quality is table stakes. The regulated wedge is everything a dev-memory or temporal-graph layer was never built to provide.

  • vs mem0mem0 is ADD-oriented, so revised facts pile up and contaminate recall. Lians excludes stale versions deterministically and adds encryption-at-rest, RBAC, and audit trails. Full comparison ↗
  • vs Zep / GraphitiThe knowledge graph is strong but lacks access control, multi-tenancy, audit, and compliance by design. Lians keeps the graph on an open compliance spine. Full comparison ↗
  • Deterministic supersessionStale suppression is keyed and reproducible — not LLM/graph invalidation that depends on extraction quality.
  • Database-layer barriersChinese-wall isolation is enforced by PostgreSQL RLS, verified in CI against a non-superuser role — not an application check that a bug can bypass.
